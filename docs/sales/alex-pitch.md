## Beats

**1. Ask about him.** New role at Starian (started January). The sabbatical. Be curious.

**2. Transition.** He already knows I'm working on something — I told him over WhatsApp a few weeks ago. Pick it up naturally: "Remember what I mentioned? Let me show you where it's at."

**3. Frame it.** Personal itch, not a market thesis. Our billing cycle is spreadsheets, PDFs, WhatsApp. I'm a software engineer — I got tired of the friction and built a shared billing workspace where landlord and tenants see the same statement, understand every charge, and close the month cleanly.

**4. Demo on laptop.** Keep it short. Show: property setup, charges with splits, draft statement, completeness warnings. Real software, not a pitch deck.

**5. Value.**
- Him: set up charges once, review-and-publish workflow, possibly hours of admin gone, fewer WhatsApp threads, clean monthly record
- Me: see what I owe and why, flag issues properly, history of every month
- Both: shared transparent space, less friction

**6. The ask.** "Let's try this on our unit. You'd be my first landlord user — I'm already the first tenant. Real bills, real cycles. Your feedback shapes what I build next." I'm covering all costs.

**7. Where this can go.** I've thought this out. Brief version:
- **Near-term revenue:** flat monthly SaaS fee per property/unit. Premium add-ons for bill automation
- **Later:** payment convenience fees (Pix, card), platform fee on payment volume, AI-assisted validation, multi-property analytics package
- **Growth is built in:** landlord invites tenants, tenants invite housemates, tenants can invite their own landlords. Billing is naturally collaborative — every user brings more users
- **Strategic wedge:** the gap between "too manual" (spreadsheets) and "too much software" (enterprise PM suites). Lightweight, trust-first, bill-backed transparency

**8. If he digs deeper:**
- *Market?* — I suspect small-time landlords (1-5 properties) all do this manually. Haven't validated beyond us yet. Solving for us first, then expanding.
- *Competition?* — Brazil has Superlógica/Owli (full rental admin for imobiliárias), QuintoAndar (managed marketplace), uCondo (condo management). Global has Buildium/AppFolio/DoorLoop (broad PM suites). Utility specialists like Zego/Conservice target large multifamily. Nothing lightweight and trust-first for small landlords doing charge coordination with tenants. The real incumbent is the spreadsheet.
- *Beyond using it?* — Honest feedback. And your read on whether this could be a real business.
- *Cost to run?* — ~$45/mo infra (Supabase + Vercel). I'm covering everything.
- *How am I building it?* — Solo engineer, AI-assisted dev (Claude Code). One person shipping what used to take a team. That's part of why I can take this risk without funding.
